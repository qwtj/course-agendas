# Medical Diseases #Overview #HealthConditions
Broad category encompassing any abnormal condition adversely affecting the structure or function of an organism, not due to immediate external injury. Includes illnesses, disorders, injuries, syndromes, infections, etc.

## Classification and Nomenclature #Taxonomy #ICD #Terminology
The systematic arrangement of diseases into groups based on shared characteristics. Essential for diagnosis, treatment, statistics, and research.
### Basis of Classification #Criteria #Methods
Diseases can be classified in various ways.
#### By Etiology (Cause) #Causation #Origin
Grouping based on the primary cause.
##### Infectious Diseases #Pathogens #Communicable
Caused by pathogenic microorganisms (bacteria, viruses, fungi, parasites).
##### Genetic Diseases #Hereditary #DNA
Caused by abnormalities in genes or chromosomes.
##### Environmental Diseases #Exposure #Toxins
Caused by physical, chemical, or biological factors in the environment.
##### Nutritional Deficiency Diseases #Diet #Malnutrition
Caused by inadequate intake of essential nutrients.
##### Physiological/Functional Diseases #Dysfunction #Metabolic
Resulting from malfunction of organs or systems (often without structural change initially). Examples include many metabolic diseases.
##### Immunological Diseases #ImmuneSystem #Hypersensitivity #Autoimmunity
Resulting from dysfunction of the immune system (e.g., allergies, autoimmune disorders, immunodeficiency).
##### Neoplastic Diseases #Cancer #Tumors
Characterized by abnormal, uncontrolled cell growth.
##### Degenerative Diseases #Aging #WearAndTear
Resulting from progressive deterioration of tissues or organs.
##### Idiopathic Diseases #UnknownCause
Diseases with no known cause.
#### By Pathophysiology #Mechanism #Process
Grouping based on the underlying functional changes or mechanisms of disease.
##### Inflammation #ImmuneResponse #TissueReaction
Diseases characterized by inflammatory processes.
##### Neoplasia #CellGrowth #Tumorigenesis
Diseases involving abnormal cell proliferation.
##### Degeneration #CellDeath #TissueDamage
Diseases involving breakdown of cells and tissues.
##### Vascular Disorders #BloodFlow #Circulation
Diseases affecting blood vessels and circulation.
##### Immunopathology #Autoimmunity #Allergy
Diseases caused by aberrant immune responses.
#### By Body System/Organ (Anatomic/Topographic) #Anatomy #Location
Grouping based on the primary organ or system affected. This is a common approach in medical specialties.
##### Cardiovascular Diseases #Heart #BloodVessels
##### Respiratory Diseases #Lungs #Airways
##### Gastrointestinal Diseases #DigestiveSystem #Gut
##### Neurological Diseases #Brain #Nerves
##### Musculoskeletal Diseases #Bones #Muscles #Joints
##### Endocrine Diseases #Hormones #Glands
##### Renal and Urogenital Diseases #Kidney #UrinaryTract #Reproductive
##### Hematologic Diseases #Blood #BoneMarrow
##### Dermatologic Diseases #Skin #Hair #Nails
##### Diseases of Sensory Organs #Eye #Ear
##### Mental and Behavioral Disorders #Psychiatry #Psychology
#### By Disease Course #Chronicity #Progression
##### Acute Diseases #ShortTerm #SuddenOnset
Rapid onset, short duration (e.g., common cold).
##### Chronic Diseases #LongTerm #Persistent
Long duration (months/years), often progressive or with remissions/relapses (e.g., diabetes, arthritis).
##### Subacute Diseases #Intermediate
Intermediate duration/severity between acute and chronic.
##### Latent Diseases #Dormant #Asymptomatic
Infection or condition present but without active symptoms (may become active later, e.g., latent TB, herpes zoster).
#### By Burden #Epidemiology #Impact
##### Communicable vs. Non-Communicable #Transmission #Lifestyle
Distinction based on whether the disease can spread between individuals.
##### Endemic/Epidemic/Pandemic #Prevalence #Spread
Classifying based on geographic prevalence and rate of spread.
##### Rare Diseases #OrphanDiseases #LowPrevalence
Diseases affecting a small percentage of the population.
### Standardized Systems #Coding #Standards
Formal systems used globally for consistency in reporting and statistics.
#### International Classification of Diseases (ICD) #WHO #Statistics
Global standard by the World Health Organization for diagnostic health information (mortality/morbidity statistics, epidemiology, clinical use). Currently ICD-11.
##### ICD Structure (e.g., ICD-11 Chapters) #Organization #Codes
Hierarchical structure with chapters covering broad disease categories (e.g., Neoplasms, Mental disorders, Infectious diseases). Uses alphanumeric codes.
#### SNOMED CT #Nomenclature #ClinicalTerms
Systematized Nomenclature of Medicine - Clinical Terms. A comprehensive clinical terminology system.
#### Other Classifications #Specialty #Specific
(e.g., DSM for mental disorders, TNM for cancer staging)
